Quick Verdict: Is SaleHoo Worth It?

SaleHoo is a legitimate and established directory for finding wholesale and dropshipping suppliers. It's not a scam, but it's also not a magic solution that finds suppliers for you. Its real value is in vetting suppliers to reduce the risk of scams, which is a major problem when sourcing online. You still have to do the work of contacting suppliers, negotiating, and testing products.

Bottom Line: If you're serious about starting a wholesale or dropshipping business and want to avoid getting scammed by fake suppliers, the annual fee is a smart insurance policy. If you're just casually browsing or expect the platform to do all the work, you'll be disappointed.

What Is SaleHoo, Really?

SaleHoo is a paid online directory that lists pre-vetted wholesale and dropshipping suppliers. Think of it as a "Yellow Pages" for product sourcing, but where every business listed has been checked for legitimacy.

Its core promise isn't to make you rich overnight, but to solve one specific, painful problem: the high risk of fraud when finding suppliers online. Instead of Googling "wholesale suppliers" and hoping for the best, you pay SaleHoo to have done that basic legitimacy checking for you.

How Does SaleHoo Actually Work?

It's a straightforward, three-part system:

  1. Pay for Access: You buy an annual membership (or occasionally a lifetime deal) to get into their member's area.
  2. Search the Directory: You use their search tools to find suppliers in your niche (e.g., "pet supplies," "home decor"). Each listing provides contact info and sometimes basic details like minimum order quantities.
  3. Do the Real Work (This is the catch): You then contact the suppliers yourself via email or phone. You negotiate prices, ask for samples, discuss shipping, and set up terms. SaleHoo gives you the list, but you build the business relationship.

They also offer a "Market Research Lab" tool to analyze product trends and a separate "SaleHoo Dropship" app for Shopify integration, but the core product is the supplier directory.

The Real Pros and Cons (No Fluff)

The Advantages (What You Actually Get)

  • Scam Prevention is the #1 Benefit: This is the primary reason to join. They weed out the blatant scams and fake "wholesalers" that are rampant online, saving you from losing money on deposits for inventory that never arrives.
  • Massive Time Saver for Initial Research: Instead of spending weeks verifying dozens of suppliers, you start with a pre-filtered list. It accelerates the initial "discovery" phase.
  • Educational Resources for Beginners: Their training materials, forums, and guides are genuinely helpful if you're new to importing, wholesale, or dropshipping. It's like a starter course.
  • Low-Cost Entry for Serious Searchers: At around $67/year, it's a relatively small investment compared to the potential loss from dealing with one fraudulent supplier.
  • Active Community & Support: Having access to other members and support can help when you hit a dead end or have a niche question.

The Disadvantages (What You Must Know)

  • It's Just a Directory, Not a Service: This is the biggest misconception. SaleHoo won't source products for you, negotiate on your behalf, or handle any logistics. You are still 100% responsible for building supplier relationships.
  • Supplier Quality Varies: "Vetted" means they're likely real businesses, not that they're the best, cheapest, or most reliable. You still must order samples, check quality, and test communication.
  • Information Can Be Outdated: Suppliers change policies, go out of business, or raise prices. The directory isn't always updated in real-time.
  • No Direct "Buy Now" Button: Unlike platforms like Spocket or Oberlo, you can't instantly import products to your store. It's a research tool first.
  • Recurring Fee: It's a subscription. If you find a great supplier in month one, you're still paying for the rest of the year to maintain access to the directory and tools.

Who Is SaleHoo REALLY For? (And Who It's Not)

SaleHoo IS a good fit for:

  • Beginners to wholesale/dropshipping who are overwhelmed and scared of getting scammed. The education and vetted list provide a safe starting point.
  • Established online sellers looking to diversify their supplier base or find new product lines with more confidence.
  • People selling in physical locations (brick-and-mortar stores, market stalls) who want to find legitimate wholesalers for inventory.
  • Anyone for whom a $70 loss from a scam is a bigger deal than a $70 annual membership fee.

SaleHoo is likely NOT a good fit for:

  • People looking for a "done-for-you" dropshipping system with automated order fulfillment. Look at Spocket, Dsers, or Zendrop instead.
  • Sellers who only want to source from super platforms like AliExpress. SaleHoo's value is in finding suppliers outside of those massive marketplaces.
  • Anyone unwilling to send emails, make calls, and negotiate with suppliers directly. If you hate outreach, this isn't the tool for you.
  • Shoestring budget startups where every dollar counts and who are willing to accept the higher risk of finding free suppliers on their own.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is SaleHoo a scam? Does it really work?

A: No, it's not a scam. It's a real company with a real service. Whether it "works" depends on your effort. It gives you a verified list of suppliers. It "works" if you use that list to contact and secure deals with those suppliers. It doesn't work if you expect the deals to be handed to you.

Q: Can I really get a refund?

A: Yes, their 60-day money-back guarantee is legitimate. This is a key feature. It allows you to test if their directory has suppliers relevant to you. If you search for your niche and find nothing useful within 60 days, you can get a full refund. This makes trying it a low-risk decision.

Q: What's the difference between SaleHoo and AliExpress?

A: They solve different problems. AliExpress is a marketplace where you can buy single items at low prices, often with slow shipping. SaleHoo is a directory of suppliers (many of whom are NOT on AliExpress) with whom you can establish direct accounts, potentially get better pricing for bulk, and access unique products. SaleHoo is for building a brand; AliExpress is often for testing.

Q: Are the suppliers really reliable?

A: "Reliable" in this context means "not an outright scam." SaleHoo vets that they are real businesses. It does not guarantee they will have the best prices, fastest shipping, or perfect communication. You must vet them further yourself by ordering samples and testing the relationship.

Q: Is the "lifetime deal" worth it?

A: Only if you plan to be in e-commerce for many years. The one-time fee for lifetime access is usually 2-3x the annual fee. If you'll use the directory for more than 2-3 years, it's mathematically worth it. If you're unsure, stick with the annual plan.
