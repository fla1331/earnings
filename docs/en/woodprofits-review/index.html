Quick Verdict: Is WoodProfits Worth It?

WoodProfits is a legitimate and comprehensive guide for starting a woodworking business. It's not a scam, but it's also not a magical "get-rich-quick" scheme. It provides real value through detailed project plans and business advice, especially for complete beginners. Your success will depend 100% on your effort to build and sell the projects.

Bottom Line: If you're serious about turning woodworking into income and need step-by-step guidance, it's a worthwhile investment. If you're looking for easy money without work, look elsewhere.

What Is WoodProfits, Really?

WoodProfits is a digital training program created by Jim Morgan. It's marketed as an "A to Z blueprint" for building a profitable home-based woodworking business. Unlike just a collection of plans, it combines project blueprints with business training on marketing, pricing, and sales.

The core promise is to help you skip the trial-and-error phase by providing plans for items that are proven to sell and teaching you how to sell them effectively, even with limited tools or experience.

How Does The WoodProfits System Work?

The program is structured as a series of steps, delivered digitally:

  1. Access the Library: You get instant access to a members' area with hundreds of woodworking plans, categorized by type and difficulty.
  2. Learn the Business Basics: Modules cover how to legally set up, price your work, calculate profit margins, and manage costs.
  3. Build from the Plans: Each plan includes a materials list, cut diagrams, and step-by-step instructions.
  4. Sell Using Their Methods: This is the key differentiator. It teaches multiple sales channels: online marketplaces (Etsy, Facebook), craft fairs, and word-of-mouth.

Who Is WoodProfits REALLY For? (And Who It's Not)

WoodProfits IS a good fit for:

  • Total beginners to woodworking who want a clear path to follow.
  • Hobbyists who love woodworking and are now curious about making some money from it.
  • People who feel overwhelmed by the business side (pricing, selling) and want it explained simply.
  • Anyone who prefers a structured, step-by-step program over piecing together free information online.

WoodProfits is likely NOT a good fit for:

  • Experienced professional woodworkers looking for advanced, unique plans.
  • Anyone expecting passive income or money without physically making and selling items.
  • People who already have a successful woodworking business and sales system in place.

Final Verdict & My Recommendation

After a detailed analysis, WoodProfits stands out as a legitimate and valuable resource for its target audience: the aspiring woodworker-entrepreneur.

Its greatest strength is combining actionable project plans with practical business knowledge in one package. It saves you significant time and helps you avoid common early mistakes.

The final call is simple: If you have the drive to work with your hands and follow the steps laid out, WoodProfits provides the map. Consider it an investment in your education as a craftsperson and business owner. The potential return from your first few successful projects can easily outweigh the initial cost.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is WoodProfits a scam? Does it really work?

A: No, it is not a scam. It is a real educational product. Whether it "works" is up to you. It provides the knowledge and plans, but you must apply them. Many users have built businesses using it, but there are no guaranteed results.

Q: I'm a complete beginner with no tools. Can I start?

A: Yes, but start small. The program suggests beginner projects that require minimal tools. The initial investment would be for both the program and some basic tools. It teaches you how to start cost-effectively.

Q: How much money can I realistically make?

A: Income varies wildly. It depends on the time you invest, the projects you choose, your skill speed, and how well you market. Some treat it as a side income, while others grow it into a full-time business. Manage your expectations and focus on learning first.

Q: What's the catch? What are people complaining about?

A: The most common "catch" is unrealistic expectations. Some buyers think purchasing the program alone will generate money. When they realize it requires real work, they feel disappointed. Other complaints are about the digital-only format or wanting more advanced plans.

Q: Is the information outdated?

A: The core principles of woodworking and selling handmade goods do not change quickly. While specific website references or platform rules might age, the fundamental business strategies and project plans remain valid. The program is periodically updated.
